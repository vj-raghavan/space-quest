About Space Quest

Space Quest is a free, ad-free learning game for children, playable in any web browser with nothing to install and no account to create. It was built by a dad for his own two kids, and it's designed to encourage rather than punish: there are no leaderboards, wrong answers teach instead of just buzzing, and practice is never blocked.

What kids can practise

  • Times tables & division — adaptive practice that focuses on the facts each child finds tricky.
  • Addition & subtraction — from single digits up to three-digit numbers.
  • Fractions — reading, simplifying, adding and subtracting.
  • Telling the time — reading analogue clocks, from o'clock to the exact minute.
  • Spelling — spot-the-correct-spelling and build-the-word games, plus your own weekly school word list.
  • Story problems, estimation, sequences and comparing numbers.
  • Music maths, angles & turns, and brain-teaser puzzles.
  • A picture-and-tap creature quiz for younger children who aren't reading yet.

Who it's for

Space Quest suits children roughly aged 5 to 10 (UK Key Stage 1 and Key Stage 2 / US kindergarten through grade 5), and works well for home learning, homeschooling and classroom warm-ups. Children earn stars and coins, keep daily streaks, and explore a galaxy of planets — each one a different skill to master.

Free to play · No ads · No sign-up · Works on phones, tablets and computers.
